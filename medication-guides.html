Fever/Pain Medication Guides

Use these quick references to view common fever/pain medication brand options for infants and children.

Always double-check the medication concentration and confirm dosing with your pediatrician or pharmacist.

Acetaminophen [160 mg / 5 mL]

Safety Tips

  • Do not exceed 6 doses in 24 hours.
  • <6 months: dose every 4 hours as needed.
  • >6 months: dose every 6 hours as needed.
  • Consider alternating acetaminophen and ibuprofen (each every 6 hours) to allow a dose every 3 hours.
  • Contact a healthcare provider for children under 2 months with fever or for persistent fever >3 days.
  • Keep a log of medication, time, and dose to avoid repeat dosing.

Children's Ibuprofen [100 mg / 5 mL]

Standard children's suspension dosed using an oral syringe or medicine cup. Verify strength before dosing.

Infant Ibuprofen [50 mg / 1.25 mL]

Safety Tips

  • Do not give to infants <6 months unless directed by a doctor.
  • Allow at least 6 hours between doses.
  • Do not exceed 4 doses in 24 hours.
  • Give with food or milk to lessen stomach upset.
