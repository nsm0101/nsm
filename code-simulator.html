PALS Code Simulator

First-person, real-time mock codes — you are the team leader at the foot of the bed. Give your orders out loud; your team executes, closes the loop, and the rhythm responds in real time. Built on the AHA / PALS Pediatric Cardiac Arrest Algorithm.

How to run the code

  1. Hold the talk button (or enable hands-free) and speak orders naturally — e.g. “Attach the monitor, start compressions.”
  2. Drive the algorithm: charge → shock → resume CPR, give epinephrine, get IO access, secure the airway, and work the reversible causes.
  3. Your team answers with closed-loop read-backs and the recorder tracks every event and the 2-minute cycles.
  4. Reassess at each rhythm check; manage well and you’ll get ROSC, then a full debrief.
Best in Chrome or Edge for voice recognition. A microphone-permission prompt will appear. No mic? You can type every order. Headphones recommended so the team’s voices don’t feed back into the mic.

For training and rehearsal only. Not a substitute for an accredited PALS course or for clinical judgment. Doses are illustrative and weight-based per PALS — always verify against your institution’s references.
